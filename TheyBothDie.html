They Both Die At The end

Adam Silvera

"They Both Die at the End" is a young adult fiction novel by Adam Silvera, published in 2017. The story follows two teenagers, Mateo Torrez and Rufus Emeterio, who receive a phone call from Death-Cast, a company that can predict
when someone will die within the next 24 hours.

Synopsis

This book consists of two parts. The first part is narrated from the perspective of a student activist named Laut, detailing how Laut and his friends devise plans, move from place to place in hiding, and ultimately get captured by secret forces. The second part is narrated by Asmara, Laut's sibling, representing the feelings of families of victims of forced disappearances, and how they search for their loved ones who never return. This book is written as a tribute to the activists who were abducted, those who returned, and those who never did, as well as the families who continue to seek answers to this day. The novel serves as a fictional embodiment that we, as Indonesians, must not forget the history that has shaped and anchored our nation. It also invites readers to uncover the mysteries of this nation, which are not taught in schools. Although this novel is a work of fiction, "The Sea Speaks" shows readers that this country has experienced dark periods in its governance.
